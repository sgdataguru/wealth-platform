## Risk Mitigation

### Patent Rejection Risk

**Risk**: Indian Patent Office rejects IP-01 or IP-02 due to Section 3(k) (business methods)

**Mitigation**:

1. **Strong Technical Framing**: Emphasize system architecture, algorithms, data processing
2. **Precedent Citations**: *Ferid Allani*, *Yahoo Inc.*, *Telefonaktiebolaget* (favorable Indian case law)
3. **Fallback Strategy**: If India rejects, file in US (stronger software patentability), then re-assess India
4. **Trade Secret Protection**: Even if patents rejected, trade secrets remain protected

**Probability**: **Low** (IP-01 and IP-02 have strong technical effect, low Section 3(k) risk)

---

### Competitor Workaround Risk

**Risk**: Competitor reads our patents and designs around them

**Mitigation**:

1. **Broad Claims**: Draft claims to cover variations and alternatives
2. **Continuation Applications**: File additional patents for improvements
3. **Trade Secret Layer**: Exact calibration values remain secret (hard to replicate performance)
4. **First-Mover Advantage**: 18-24 months head start (time to build market share)

**Probability**: **Medium** (patents are public, but trade secrets provide additional protection)

---

### Trade Secret Leakage Risk

**Risk**: Employee or contractor leaks calibration values or ML models

**Mitigation**:

1. **Legal**: NDAs with liquidated damages clauses
2. **Technical**: Access controls, encryption, audit logs, watermarking
3. **Organizational**: Need-to-know basis, security training, exit interviews
